Privacy Policy

Twofold · Last updated 2 July 2026

What we collect

Twofold stores the minimum a small paper needs to print:

  • Your account: a user ID from Sign in with Apple, your chosen username, and the name you want on your byline.
  • Your stories: the two words you file each day, any one-sentence answers you write, and the time you filed.
  • Your dateline: a place name (like "Greenpoint") that appears in your byline. If you allow location access, we look up your neighbourhood name at filing time. Coordinates are used for that lookup and immediately discarded; only the name is stored, and you can type your own instead.
  • Your connections: who your correspondents are, requests, quiet "?" asks, and any blocks or reports you make.

What we don't do

  • No advertising, no trackers, no analytics beyond crash reporting.
  • No selling or sharing of your data with third parties.
  • No public profiles: your stories are visible only to correspondents you accept.

Where it lives

Data is stored with Supabase (hosted Postgres) and protected by row-level security: the database itself enforces that only you and your accepted correspondents can read your stories. Nobody can see who asked about a story, only the author's count.

Deleting your account

The Desk → Delete account removes your account and every story, ask, and connection with it, immediately and permanently.
